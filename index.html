Musical Instruments

The evolution of the musical instruments

Music is a form of art, which originates from the Greek word meaning "art of the Muses." In ancient Greece, the Muses were the goddesses who inspired the arts, such as literature, music, and poetry.

Music has been played since the dawn of human history using instruments and through vocal singing. Although it is uncertain how and when the first musical instrument was invented, most historians point to early flutes made from animal bones that are at least 37,000 years old. The oldest known written song dates back 4,000 years and was written in ancient cuneiform script.

Instruments were created to produce musical sounds. Any object that produces sound can be considered a musical instrument, especially if it is designed for that purpose. Take a look at the various instruments that have appeared over the centuries from different parts of the world.

Accordion

A fascinating and versatile musical instrument

The accordion is a portable, box-shaped musical instrument that produces sound by pushing air through reeds using a bellows. It is played by compressing or expanding the bellows while pressing buttons or keys, which open valves to allow air to flow across metal reeds tuned to specific pitches. The accordion is used in a wide variety of musical genres, including folk, classical, jazz, and popular music, and has a strong cultural presence in regions such as Europe, South America, and North America. It typically comes in two main types: the piano accordion, which has a keyboard similar to a piano, and the button accordion, which uses buttons for both melody and bass.

  • Inventor: Friedrich Buschmann
  • Inception Year: 1822

Clarinet

A single-reed musical instrument in the woodwind family

The clarinet is a woodwind instrument with a single-reed mouthpiece, a straight cylindrical body, and a flared bell. It is typically made of wood or plastic and has a wide range of notes and dynamic expression, making it a versatile instrument used in classical, jazz, and contemporary music. The clarinet produces sound when the player blows air through the mouthpiece, causing the reed to vibrate. It has a system of tone holes and keys that the player operates with their fingers to change the pitch. The most common type is the B♭ clarinet, but there are several other types in different keys, including the A and bass clarinets.

  • Inventor: Johann Denner
  • Inception Year: Late 1600s

Contrabasse

A majestic instrument that has played a pivotal role in orchestras, jazz bands, and various musical genres for centuries

The Contrabasse, also known as the upright bass or contrabass, is the largest and lowest-pitched bowed string instrument in the orchestra. It typically has four strings and is played either by plucking (pizzicato) or using a bow (arco). Due to its deep, rich tones, it plays a foundational role in classical music ensembles, jazz bands, and various other music genres. The double bass is unique among the string family in that it is tuned in fourths, unlike the violin, viola, and cello, which are tuned in fifths.

  • Inventor: Domenico Dragonetti
  • Inception Year: 1516

Pipe Organ

One of the oldest instruments still used in European classical music

The pipe organ is a grand and complex keyboard instrument that produces sound by forcing air through pipes selected by the player via keyboards and pedalboard. Each pipe corresponds to a specific pitch and timbre, and the organ can have thousands of pipes arranged in ranks. It is often found in churches, cathedrals, and concert halls due to its powerful and majestic sound. The pipe organ is known for its versatility and ability to sustain notes indefinitely, making it a central instrument in liturgical and classical music.

  • Inventor: Ctesibius of Alexandria
  • Inception Year: 3rd century BC

Guitar

A stringed musical instrument that is usually fretted and typically has six or twelve strings

The guitar is a popular stringed instrument typically with six strings, played by strumming or plucking with the fingers or a pick. It is used in a wide range of musical styles, from classical and flamenco to rock, blues, and pop. Guitars can be acoustic, using a hollow body to amplify the sound naturally, or electric, relying on electronic amplification. The instrument is known for its expressiveness, portability, and versatility, and it plays both melodic and harmonic roles in music.

  • Inventor: people of Malaga
  • Inception Year: 15th century

Piano

A keyboard instrument that produces sound when its keys are depressed, activating an action mechanism where hammers strike strings

The piano is a widely used keyboard instrument that produces sound by striking strings with hammers when keys are pressed. It has 88 keys, allowing for a broad range of notes and dynamics, making it suitable for solo, ensemble, and orchestral music. The piano can play both melody and harmony simultaneously and is used in nearly every musical genre, including classical, jazz, pop, and film music. Its ability to convey a wide range of emotions and styles has made it one of the most important instruments in Western music.

  • Inventor: Bartolomeo Cristofori
  • Inception Year: 1700s
